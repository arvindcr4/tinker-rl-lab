\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Reinforcement learning post-training now defines the state of the art in
language-model reasoning and alignment~\citep{ouyang2022training,%
guo2025deepseekr1nature}, and a small zoo of algorithms
(PPO~\citep{schulman2017proximal}, GRPO~\citep{shao2024deepseekmath},
DPO~\citep{rafailov2024direct}) interacts with a larger zoo of training
frameworks (TRL, \tinker{}, OpenRLHF~\citep{hu2024openrlhf},
veRL~\citep{sheng2024verl}).  Every week a new benchmark reports that one
algorithm ``beats'' another, usually on a different model family, library, and
reward protocol.  What the community lacks is not another leaderboard but a
controlled empirical substrate that can say \emph{when} and \emph{why} RL
post-training succeeds or fails---the same diagnosis Henderson et
al.~\citep{henderson2018deep} demanded of classical deep RL nearly a decade
ago.

\ours{} is that substrate: 79 controlled runs spanning 7 RL libraries,
5 model families, 32+ checkpoints, and scales from 0.6B to $\sim$1T
parameters, all reported against \texttt{rliable}
standards~\citep{agarwal2021deep} with stratified bootstrap 95\% CIs,
IQM aggregation, and pre-registered hypotheses.  Every cross-library
comparison holds model, task, reward, and seed fixed; every cross-family
comparison holds library and hyperparameter budget fixed.  Running this
substrate produced five empirical discoveries that reframe what practitioners
should measure, report, and optimize.

\paragraph{F1 --- ZVF as an early-warning diagnostic.}
The Zero-Variance Fraction (ZVF)---the share of GRPO groups whose rollouts all
receive identical rewards and therefore contribute zero gradient---correlates
with final reward across our corpus
($r_{\text{Pearson}}{=}{-}0.769$, $p{=}0.0008$; Spearman
$\rho{=}{-}0.784$, $p{=}0.0005$).  However, a lagged regression analysis
(\S\ref{sec:zvf-lagged}) reveals that this zero-order correlation is largely
explained by current reward level: the partial correlation of ZVF$_t$ with
reward$_{t+5}$ controlling for reward$_t$ is only $r{=}0.059$ ($p{=}0.096$),
though ZVF retains marginal independent signal at longer horizons (lag 10:
partial $r{=}0.080$, $p{=}0.045$).  ZVF's practical value is as a
\emph{triage diagnostic}: task type alone drives most of the variance
(tool-use without SFT sits at ZVF $\approx$ 100\% from step 0, while GSM8K
averages 8.5\%), and all runs with sustained ZVF $\geq 80\%$ and reward
$\leq 5\%$ in the first 5 steps collapsed.  The simultaneous emergence of
at least six independent 2025--2026 proposals targeting this failure
mode~\citep{zhang2026aero,zhang2026greso} externally validates that ZVF
names a real, cross-scale pathology.

\paragraph{F2 --- Inverted-U in group size.}
Holding model, task, seed, and LR fixed on Qwen3-8B/GSM8K, group size $G$
traces an inverted-U in gradient utilization: $G{=}4$ collapses to $23.8\%$
last-10 with immediate ZVF saturation; $G{=}16$ recovers; $G{=}32$ maximizes
mean gradient utilization ($54.5\%$) and delays saturation onset to step 29;
$G{=}64$ yields diminishing returns at $2\times$ compute.  ``More rollouts is
always better'' is wrong---past $G{=}32$, additional samples buy variance
reduction the advantage estimator cannot spend, a finding consistent with the
scaling-law analysis of Zhou et al.~\citep{zhou2026demystify} and the
2-GRPO efficiency argument of Wu et al.~\citep{wu2025grpo_dpo}.

\paragraph{F3 --- Base vs.\ instruct dichotomy.}
The delta between SFT-aligned and raw base checkpoints ($+0.922$ on GSM8K:
$0.047 \to 0.969$) dwarfs every RL contribution we measure.  The dichotomy
holds cross-family (Qwen, Llama) and cross-architecture (dense, MoE):
Qwen3-30B-A3B-Instruct reaches 100\% last-10 while its base-model twin stalls
at 32.5\% under identical GRPO protocols.  Below 8B-instruct, both Qwen and
Llama collapse to ZVF-saturated non-learning.  The RL community's implicit
``GRPO makes models reason'' framing inverts the causal arrow: SFT gates
trainability, GRPO refines what SFT has already unlocked.

\paragraph{F4 --- PPO vs.\ GRPO may be model-dependent (hypothesis).}
At matched compute on Modal H100 GPUs, GRPO outperforms PPO on Qwen3-8B
(last-10: $34.4\%$ vs.\ $22.5\%$) while PPO outperforms GRPO on
Llama-3.1-8B ($97.5\%$ vs.\ $84.4\%$; Mann-Whitney $r{=}0.94$,
$p{<}10^{-10}$ on per-step rewards).  The direction of advantage
\emph{reverses} across families.  However, these comparisons are single-seed
and span only two model families; with MDE $d{=}2.024$ at 80\% power, we
report this as a \emph{hypothesis} requiring replication rather than a
conclusion.  If confirmed, universal algorithm recommendations would be
incorrect by construction; model-conditioned selection would be required.

\paragraph{F5 --- Kimi-K2 sustained ceiling.}
Scale alone does not buy stability.  Nemotron-120B reaches peak $87.5\%$ and
then catastrophically collapses to $16.2\%$ last-10 on GSM8K, while
Kimi-K2 ($\sim$1T MoE) sustains peak $100\%$ /
last-10 $80\%$ in 20 steps, and Kimi-K2-Thinking reaches $91.7\%$ last-10.
Qwen3-235B-A22B achieves perfect $100\%$ last-10 under the same protocol.
The ceiling is neither monotone in parameter count nor predicted by the
three-phase saturation model of~\citet{nimmaturi2025scalinglaws}; it is
conditional on pre-training recipe (agentic RL, MoE routing, instruction
quality), making frontier-scale evaluation a qualitatively distinct regime.

\paragraph{F6 --- Cold-Start Capability Threshold.}
Because GRPO has no value function to estimate partial progress, it
depends entirely on the base policy spontaneously sampling at least one
correct completion within each group of size $G$.  This yields a closed-form
exploration boundary: gradient flow requires zero-shot pass rate strictly
greater than $1/G$, otherwise every sample in the group is wrong,
$\sigma_r\!\to\!\epsilon$, and advantages collapse.  Our task hierarchy
obeys this bound---Tool-Use and GSM8K (initial ZVF\,=\,0.00) learn,
MATH-500 (initial ZVF\,=\,0.20) improves partially, HumanEval
(initial ZVF\,=\,1.00) is null---and the bound cleanly resolves the
apparent contradiction with an external Qwen3-8B pipeline that solved
141/164 HumanEval problems: dense partial-credit rewards bypassed the
$1/G$ floor rather than the semantic difficulty of code.  This extends
sparse-reward exploration arguments~\citep{burda2018exploration,%
ecoffet2019goexplore} from value-based RL into the critic-free regime
and names a concrete failure boundary the original GRPO
formulation~\citep{shao2024deepseekmath} leaves implicit.

\paragraph{F7 --- Framework is a first-class experimental variable.}
Tinker, TRL, Modal PPO, and classical PPO libraries expose different
sampling loops, optimizer behaviours, rollout accounting, and KL
handling.  On matched model, task, and compute, the PPO-vs-GRPO winner
flips between Qwen3-8B and Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (F4), and TRL-GRPO
differs measurably from Tinker-GRPO under the same nominal algorithm.
Reporting ``PPO vs.\ GRPO'' without the library is therefore
under-specified.  This extends Henderson et al.'s reproducibility
critique~\citep{henderson2018deep} from Atari/MuJoCo into LLM
post-training and argues that library-level ablations belong alongside
algorithmic claims in every RLHF paper.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\textbf{(i)}~F1--F7 as the first controlled cross-library, cross-family
empirical map of RL post-training dynamics, with honest assessment of
statistical limitations.
\textbf{(ii)}~ZVF as a triage diagnostic: lagged regression confirms its
zero-order correlation is largely confounded by current reward, but ZVF
retains marginal independent signal at longer horizons and all runs meeting
the early-failure rule collapsed.
\textbf{(iii)}~A reproducibility substrate---79 runs (15 multi-seed, 28
single-seed complete, 36 partial/interrupted providing early-training
diagnostics), step-level traces, Docker environments, W\&B
logs\footnote{\url{https://wandb.ai/arvindcr4-pes-university/tinker-rl-lab-world-class}},
LoRA adapters, and HuggingFace checkpoints---that lets others falsify or
extend every finding.
\textbf{(iv)}~The $1/G$ Cold-Start Capability Threshold (F6) as a
closed-form, falsifiable boundary for when critic-free RL can produce
usable gradients, together with two pre-registered rescue experiments
(large-$G$ and dense partial rewards).
\textbf{(v)}~Framework-level ablations (F7) as evidence that library
choice is an experimental variable, not an implementation detail, for
RL post-training comparisons.
