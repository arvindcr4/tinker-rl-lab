Q16: Operational definition of agentic LLM fine-tuning
An agentic LLM fine-tuning approach involves the model learning to autonomously interact with tools or perform tasks with minimal human intervention. This contrasts with non-agentic LLMs, which may generate outputs based solely on input without performing actions or interacting with external systems (e.g., tools, APIs).

Q19: Synthetic-real gap vs. train-test reward discrepancy
The synthetic-real gap refers to the difference in distribution between synthetic training data and real-world test data, whereas the train-test reward discrepancy refers to the mismatch between the reward signal used during training and the actual test performance. To separate these, clarify that the synthetic-real gap is a data distribution issue, while the train-test reward discrepancy pertains to the metric used for evaluating performance, possibly influenced by training methodology.
