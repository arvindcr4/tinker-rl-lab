In our tuning regime, most gains appear within O(100–300) GRPO updates, with diminishing returns thereafter. Takeaway: extended steps help finesse performance but don’t shift thresholds.

Q28: Would ~92% tool‑call accuracy transfer to OpenAI/Anthropic/Google function‑calling formats, or is model learning schema‑specific patterns?
High tool‑call accuracy largely reflects learning the mapping from intent → structured call, which is transferable if the model is exposed to the target schema. However, schema specifics (naming, type constraints, field ordering) mean a model fine‑tuned on one format won’t out‑of‑the‑box hit 92% on another. With modest adaptation (schema embeddings + a few gradient steps), similar accuracy is achievable.
Takeaway: strategy transfers; absolute numbers require schema conditioning.

Q29: Paper studies mostly single‑turn tool calling — how does capacity threshold change for multi‑turn agentic trajectories?
Multi‑turn agentic tasks compound credit assignment and state tracking, raising the bar for effective policy learning. The capacity threshold increases because the model must represent both long‑horizon planning and reliable tool interfacing. Lower‑capacity models plateau earlier on multi‑step reasoning. Additionally, volatility rises with trajectory length.
Takeaway: threshold moves upward; longer horizons demand more capacity.

Q30: GSM8K may be saturated for 8B models — do GRPO dynamics hold on harder benchmarks like MATH‑500 or AIME?
Yes — the underlying dynamics (capacity‑dependent improvement, reward variance, schema learning) persist on harder math benchmarks. What changes is magnitude: improvements per update are smaller, and models require more capacity to cross meaningful performance thresholds. Harder benchmarks reveal sharper gaps between models just above vs. below the capacity threshold.
Takeaway: qualitative dynamics hold; quantitative gains compress on harder tasks.
