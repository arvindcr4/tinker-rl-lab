Q5: REWARD DESIGN

The binary vs. continuous reward design for GRPO is critical and can influence convergence rates and performance.

Binary Rewards (for Math):

Binary rewards (0/1) are simpler, leading to clear success/failure signals. They encourage faster convergence since the model directly sees whether it succeeds or fails. However, sparse rewards can be harder for GRPO to optimize, especially if the rewards are infrequent.

Continuous Rewards (for Tool Calling):

Continuous rewards (0-1) allow for fine-grained feedback, enabling the model to adjust its behavior incrementally. These rewards are often more informative for the model, leading to smoother convergence and potentially better generalization.

Statistical Implications:

Binary rewards are more likely to lead to discrete jumps in policy behavior, making them less stable but possibly faster in some cases.

Continuous rewards provide more gradual gradients, potentially offering more stable and precise convergence but possibly at the cost of slower learning.

Recommendation:

For math reasoning, binary rewards make sense due to clear success/failure outcomes.

For tool calling, continuous rewards may work better, as they provide nuanced feedback to adjust tool-usage strategies over time.
